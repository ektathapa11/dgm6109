Visualization Sketch Proposals

This project explores the relationship between smartphone usage and mental overload. The recorded data includes total screen time, number of app switches, number of notifications received, and mental overload levels measured at two different times of the day (2pm and 10pm). The goal of the visualizations is to explore how different aspects of smartphone usage may relate to increases in mental overload throughout the day.

Sketch 1 - Screen Time vs Mental Overload Scatterplot

Hypothesis:
As total screen time increases, daily average mental overload increases. A higher number of notifications may further contribute to higher overload levels.

Data Elements:

  • Total Screen Time (minutes, approximately 150-550 minutes)
  • Daily Average Mental Overload (scale 1-5)
  • App Switches (approximately 60-156 switches per day)

Representation:

  • X-axis represents total screen time in minutes.
  • Y-axis represents daily average mental overload on a scale from 1 to 5.
  • Each plotted point represents a single day in the dataset.
  • Point color represents the number of app switches (lighter color = fewer switches, darker color = more switches).

Sketch 2 - Screen Time vs Notifications Trend

Question:
Do higher screen time levels correspond to higher numbers of notifications?

Data Elements:

  • Total Screen Time (minutes, approximately 150-550 minutes)
  • Notifications received per day (approximately 45-132 notifications)
  • App Switches (approximately 60-156 switches per day)
  • Daily Average Mental Overload (scale 1-5)

Representation:

  • X-axis represents total screen time in minutes.
  • Y-axis represents number of notifications received per day.
  • Circles represent individual days in the dataset.
  • Circle size represents the number of app switches.
  • A connecting line shows the trend relationship between screen time and notifications.

Sketch 3 - Mental Overload Heatmap Grid

Question:
Does mental overload increase later in the day when smartphone activity is high?

Data Elements:

  • Date
  • Mental Overload at 2pm (scale 1-5)
  • Mental Overload at 10pm (scale 1-5)
  • Notifications received per day (approximately 45-132 notifications)

Representation:

  • Rows represent each date in the dataset.
  • Columns represent the time of day (2pm and 10pm).
  • Cell color intensity represents the level of mental overload.
  • Light shading represents low overload, medium shading represents moderate overload, and dark shading represents high overload.
  • Cell border thickness represents the number of notifications received that day.

Reference: The visualization ideas were inspired by examples from the Data Visualization Catalogue and the Dear Data project. These resources helped me understand different ways to represent multi-dimensional data visually. The scatterplot concept influenced the first sketch for exploring relationships between screen time and mental overload, while the heatmap-style grid in the third sketch was inspired by visualization techniques shown in these resources.
