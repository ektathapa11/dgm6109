Mental Overload Heatmap

Hypothesis: As app switching frequency increases, perceived mental overload increases throughout the day.

Work Explanation

This visualization explores the relationship between app switching behavior and perceived mental overload at different times of the day. The dataset includes daily records of app switches, notifications, and self-reported mental overload levels in the morning and at night. Each row represents a day, and two rectangles are shown for each day: one for morning and one for night. The width of each rectangle represents the number of app switches, while the color intensity (light to dark red) represents the level of mental overload. Darker shades indicate higher overload. This design allows for easy comparison across days and between times of the day, helping to identify patterns in how digital behavior affects mental state. Although notification data was collected, it is not visually emphasized in the current design, but it provides additional context for understanding potential sources of cognitive interruption.

Observations

  • There is a noticeable trend where days with higher app switching tend to show darker red shades, indicating higher mental overload.
  • Mental overload is often higher at night compared to the morning, suggesting that prolonged device use throughout the day may accumulate stress.
  • Days with lower app switching (e.g., Feb 13-15) show lighter shades, indicating lower mental overload.
  • High app switching days (e.g., Feb 23-24) consistently show high overload in both morning and night.

Conclusion

The visualization supports the hypothesis that increased app switching is associated with higher mental overload. While the relationship is not perfectly linear, there is a clear pattern where higher digital activity corresponds to higher perceived stress levels. Additionally, the increase in overload from morning to night suggests that cumulative usage throughout the day may contribute to mental fatigue. This highlights the potential impact of frequent app switching on cognitive well-being.
