Mental Overload Heatmap

Question: Does mental overload increase later in the day when screentime activity is high?

Work Explanation

This visualization shows the relationship between app switching and mental overload at different times of the day. The dataset includes daily information about app switches, notifications, and self-reported mental overload in the morning and at night. Each row represents one day, with two rectangles for comparison: one for the morning and one for the night. The width of each rectangle shows the number of app switches, while the color (from light to dark red) represents the level of mental overload. Darker colors indicate higher overload. This design makes it easy to compare patterns across different days and times of the day. Although notification data is included, it is not strongly highlighted in the visualization, but it still provides useful background information about possible interruptions.

Observations

  • There is a noticeable trend where days with higher app switching tend to show darker red shades, indicating higher mental overload.
  • Mental overload is often higher at night compared to the morning, suggesting that prolonged device use throughout the day may accumulate stress.
  • Days with lower app switching (e.g., Feb 13-15) show lighter shades, indicating lower mental overload.
  • High app switching days (e.g., Feb 23-24) consistently show high overload in both morning and night.
